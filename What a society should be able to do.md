# What a Society Should Be Able to Do

A society of 30,000 people is the primary unit of the Resolute Society's structure. It is large enough to sustain genuine specialization — people who spend their working lives on a single trade and train successors — but small enough that every member can know the society's leadership and have a meaningful voice in its direction.

The standard here is full functional self-sufficiency at the level of basic civilization: food, shelter, health, law, education, and the material production that supports daily life. A society should be able to meet these needs without trade or outside help, even if cooperation with other societies makes life significantly better.

This is not the same as isolation. A society that can feed itself and is choosing to trade for variety is in a completely different position from one that cannot feed itself and is depending on trade to survive. The goal is the first condition — resilience through genuine capacity, not through optimized dependence.

There are things a society cannot do alone, and those belong to the multi-society tier. This document covers what falls within the society's own capacity and what the society should actively maintain and not allow to atrophy.

---

## Feed Itself

- Produce enough calories to sustain its membership from local agriculture — not every calorie under all conditions, but enough that supply chain failure is a disruption rather than a catastrophe
- Maintain a seed library covering all staple crops, with enough variety and redundancy that a single bad season does not eliminate future planting capacity
- Raise animals for meat, dairy, eggs, and fiber
- Mill grain
- Process and preserve food at scale: canning, fermentation, cold storage, drying, smoking, salting
- Manage soil health across growing generations, not just extract from it
- Maintain the knowledge to expand food production under pressure — more land, different crops, emergency techniques — without having to learn it for the first time during a crisis

## Provide Healthcare

- Deliver primary care: treatment of common illness and injury, chronic disease management, prenatal care, pediatric care, basic diagnostics
- Perform general surgery: wound closure, fracture management, appendectomy, basic abdominal surgery, Cesarean section
- Provide dental care: examination, cleaning, extraction, basic restorative work
- Provide obstetric care: normal delivery, management of common complications
- Compound basic medicines and maintain a working pharmacopeia of locally-producible remedies — herbal, fermented, and otherwise
- Provide mental health support: counseling, crisis intervention, community-based care for members who cannot function independently
- Manage public health: sanitation, disease surveillance, outbreak containment, vaccination where vaccines are available
- Maintain enough practitioners in each discipline that no single death leaves the society without care capacity

What a society cannot sustain alone — intensive care, complex specialist surgery, pharmaceutical manufacturing, medical imaging — belongs to the multi-society tier. The society's job is to manage what is within its reach and to have clear referral relationships to the federation for what is not.

## Build and Maintain Its Built Environment

- Construct and maintain dwellings, common buildings, food storage, and basic infrastructure
- Build with locally available materials — timber, stone, earth, brick — without depending on supply chains for structural components
- Maintain water infrastructure: wells, springs, cisterns, distribution at community scale
- Manage sanitation: human waste treatment, composting, drainage, gray water management
- Maintain paths and roads within society territory, including bridges over small watercourses
- Inspect all community structures on a regular cycle and address problems before they become failures

## Produce Energy

- Generate enough power for essential community functions from local sources — wood, water, wind, solar — without depending on grid infrastructure
- Heat community buildings and dwellings through a combination of efficient construction and local fuel supply, without fossil fuel dependence as a baseline requirement
- Produce and maintain fuel supplies: charcoal, firewood, biogas where feasible
- Maintain and repair the energy systems the society depends on — not just operate them until they fail

## Make and Repair Things

A society's material self-sufficiency depends on whether it can make and repair the things it uses. Import what you can while you can; be able to make what you need when you cannot.

- **Iron and steel at bloomery scale** — smelt iron and work it into tools, hardware, fasteners, agricultural implements, structural components, and cookware. A bloomery or small blast furnace producing wrought iron and basic steel is within single-society capacity. Industrial steel at volume is not.
- **Basic glass** — make functional glass by the cylinder method: bottles, jars, drinking vessels, and flat window glass. Optical glass and scientific glassware belong to the multi-society tier.
- **Ceramics** — produce functional pottery, tile, brick, and fire-clay goods. High-fire porcelain and refractory ceramics require multi-society infrastructure.
- **Leather goods** — tan hides and produce footwear, harness, bags, belts, and work gear
- **Textiles** — full fiber-to-garment cycle: fiber processing, spinning, weaving, knitting, sewing, and repair
- **Cordage** — make rope and twine from plant fiber at the scale the society requires
- **Paper** — produce paper from plant fiber for records, education, and printing
- **Printing** — operate a printing press for books, records, and community publications
- **Woodwork** — work timber into furniture, structures, vehicles, tools, and containers
- **Basic machining** — shape and fit metal parts, cut threads, perform the mechanical work that keeps equipment functional

## Educate Its Members

- Provide universal primary and secondary education, reaching every child regardless of family circumstance
- Train practitioners in all essential trades through apprenticeship — not just teach the subject, but produce qualified successors
- Maintain a library adequate for a complete secondary education in any subject
- Preserve and transmit the knowledge base of all society divisions, in writing, in a form that could be used by someone learning the trade for the first time
- Run ongoing adult education: practical skills, civic knowledge, the content of *What a Person Should Be Able to Do*

## Govern Itself

- Conduct free, fair, and regular elections by which members choose their leadership and make collective decisions
- Maintain records: identity, births, deaths, marriages, property, contracts
- Adjudicate disputes and enforce community standards through a functioning judicial process
- Maintain law and order through organized watch capacity
- Respond to internal crises — fire, medical emergency, structural failure, crime — with organized response capacity rather than improvised reaction

## Communicate and Record

- Move information reliably within the society and to neighboring societies through a courier function
- Keep a permanent archive of community records
- Publish a community record — decisions, news, notices — on a regular cycle
- Operate communication systems that do not depend on external infrastructure
- Maintain standing contact and relationships with neighboring societies, not just emergency contact

## Care for Its Members

- Ensure no member lacks food, shelter, adequate clothing, or essential healthcare
- Provide organized support for members who cannot meet their own basic needs: elderly members, members with disabilities, children without adequate family support
- Maintain mortuary capacity and dignified death practices
- Provide crisis support — emergency housing, emergency food, emergency medical care — that can mobilize quickly when members need it

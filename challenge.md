The Society anticipates that its operations may attract regulatory attention as they grow. It welcomes that attention. Any examination of the kin circuit will find exactly what this document describes — covenant members exchanging mutual aid in a closed-loop non-commercial circuit because dollar scarcity makes dollar transactions impossible for basic needs. Any attempt to restrict or tax that activity must answer the prior question: why are these people dollar scarce? The Society's founding documents answer that question in detail. The communities the Society serves were made dollar scarce by specific historical processes — extraction, deindustrialization, the deliberate organization of local economies to export value. An authority that taxes the community's response to that extraction without addressing its cause is not closing a loophole. It is continuing the extraction by a different mechanism. The Society will make that argument publicly, specifically, and with full documentation, in any forum where the question arises.

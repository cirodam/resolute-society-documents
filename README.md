# Resolute Society

A Resolute Society is a voluntary association of persons committed to mutual aid, self-governance, and the preservation and transmission of the knowledge and skills on which a good community life depends. It is not a political party, a commercial enterprise, or an instrument of any ideology. It is a community — prior to politics, prior to markets — organized around the obligations persons bear to one another.

This repository contains the foundational and operational documents of a Resolute Society. They are organized by level of specificity, from the philosophical foundations that make everything else legible to the operational plans and regulations that govern particular domains.

---

## The Architecture

### Axioms

The axioms are what we hold to be true about the world, about persons, and about what it costs to ignore either. They are not derived from anything more fundamental — they are the foundation. Everything else in these documents follows from them, directly or by implication.

### Telos

The telos is what a Resolute Society is ultimately for — not as an organization but for the people within it. It names the kind of person the Society exists to form, describes what full orientation looks like in practice, and establishes why the community and the person are inseparable. Where the axioms say what is true, the telos says what we are building toward.

### Principles

The principles translate the axioms into commitments — specific orientations toward reality, suffering, persons, community, ecology, economy, and governance. Each principle is grounded explicitly in one or more axioms. Where the telos names the destination, the principles describe how we are obligated to see, to act, and to build in order to get there.

### Governance

The governance definition specifies what a Resolute Society must look like as an institution in order to be faithful to the principles. Where the principles say what we must be, the governance says what we must build. It is not a set of administrative rules — it is the structural expression of the commitments the principles require. A Society that does not meet these governance requirements is not a Resolute Society regardless of what it calls itself.

## How to Read These Documents

Start with the axioms. Everything else is downstream of them. The telos becomes legible once the axioms are understood — it names what we are building toward given what we hold to be true. The principles become legible once the telos is understood. The governance becomes legible once the principles are understood. The master plans, regulations, and curriculum become legible once the governance is understood.

The essays engage questions that the surrounding culture has captured with partisan framing. The Society does not argue within that framing — it examines the frame. The essays apply the axioms and principles honestly to contested questions, following the evidence and the logic wherever they lead, without deference to any political tribe.

The curriculum documents represent what a Resolute Society believes every member deserves access to — not as enrichment but as a condition of dignity and genuine self-governance.

The master plans are operational. They describe how the Society fulfills its obligations in each domain of material life. They are intended to be practical enough to act on and principled enough to be worth acting on.

# Education — Phase 2.1: Practical Skills

## What It Is

Practical skills are the hands-on curriculum that the classroom space enables at a scale the tutor program cannot. These are courses where demonstration matters — where you have to see something done before you can do it yourself, and where the act of doing it alongside other people is part of how it's learned.

The knowledge that gets taught here already exists in the Society's membership. Warren has electricians, carpenters, plumbers, shade-tree mechanics, experienced cooks, gardeners, and people who have been preserving food for decades. The course is the mechanism that moves that knowledge from the individual who holds it to the community that needs it — and compensates the holder for teaching.

---

## Home Repair

Basic carpentry, basic plumbing, basic electrical — the work a careful non-specialist can do safely, stopping short of what requires a license or creates safety liability. How to patch drywall. How to fix a slow drain. How to replace an outlet. How to identify what you're looking at in a crawl space. Understanding how systems work so you know when you can handle something yourself and when you need to call someone licensed.

This course runs in direct connection with the housing lane. The member who triages houses needs to know what she's looking at. The member whose house gets triaged may learn to address some of what was found. The knowledge that was implicit in the triage work — held by whoever did the walking-through — becomes explicit and transmissible in the course.

The trades knowledge in Warren is held by people in their 50s and 60s who retired when the mills closed or whose trade employment disappeared. It is at risk of dying with them. The course is how it doesn't.

## Vehicle Maintenance

In a city where 74.6% of workers drive alone to work and public transit is minimal, a vehicle is often the difference between employment and unemployment. Basic maintenance — oil changes, tire rotation, fluid checks, reading warning lights, understanding what a mechanic is telling you when they describe a problem — keeps cars running and prevents the cascading financial crisis that follows a breakdown.

A vehicle maintenance course works best with a car present. A driveway or a parking area adjacent to the Society's space. A member who knows engines demonstrating on an actual engine. The classroom component — understanding what the warning light means, how to evaluate whether a repair estimate is reasonable — runs inside. The hands-on component runs outside.

## Food Preparation from Staples

The bulk dry goods the store carries — rice, beans, lentils, oats, flour, cornmeal — require knowledge to cook well. A practical cooking course teaches how to turn these staples into actual meals. It closes the loop between what the store sells and what members can eat.

This course runs in the community kitchen once Phase 2 of the food lane is established. Before that, it runs in whatever kitchen space the Society can access. The cooking knowledge of Warren's Black community — the bean dishes, the greens, the slow-cooked proteins, the cornbreads — belongs in this curriculum. The elder who has been cooking this way her whole life is the teacher. The ledger pays her.

---

## Food Preservation

Canning, freezing, fermenting, drying. These courses connect directly to the gleaning work in the food lane: you cannot bring home a hundred pounds of tomatoes without knowing what to do with them. Teaching preservation transforms what would otherwise spoil into months of stored food.

The August gleaning party that ends with members canning together is simultaneously a food lane operation and an education lane course. Both earn credits. Both produce something real.

---

## Gardening

Starting a garden, maintaining soil, growing for yield in Ohio's growing season, knowing what produces in this climate. This course is the on-ramp to the Agriculture lane's work on vacant lots and farm partnerships. Members who know how to grow things are what makes land productive once the Society has access to it.

The gardening course is most valuable taught by someone who has grown in Trumbull County specifically — who knows the soil, the drainage, the frost dates, the pests, what varieties perform. That knowledge is in the membership or close to it.

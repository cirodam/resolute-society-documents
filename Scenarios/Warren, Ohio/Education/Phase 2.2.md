# Education — Phase 2.2: Systems Navigation

## What It Is

Systems navigation is the knowledge of what exists outside the Society and how to access it. Government programs, legal resources, healthcare navigation, tenant rights — the infrastructure that is technically available to Warren's residents and that most of them underuse because navigating it requires knowledge they don't have and time they barely have.

This is not glamorous curriculum. It does not produce a skill you can demonstrate in a driveway. What it produces is money, stability, and protection — in the form of benefits claimed, bills reduced, legal options understood, and crises avoided because someone knew what to do before the situation became irreversible.

---

## Benefits Enrollment

**HEAP and PIPP Plus.** Ohio's utility assistance programs are among the most underused benefits available to Warren's residents. HEAP provides direct utility bill relief for households up to 175% of the federal poverty level. PIPP Plus caps monthly utility payments as a percentage of income and prevents shutoffs for enrolled customers. Many eligible households in Warren are not enrolled in either. The course teaches who qualifies, how to apply, what documentation is needed, and how to handle the renewal process.

**SNAP.** The enrollment and renewal process for food assistance is not complicated but has enough steps that people miss them, miss deadlines, or lose benefits through procedural failures rather than ineligibility. Teaching members to navigate SNAP enrollment — and to help other members through it — keeps benefits flowing to people who are entitled to them.

**Medicaid.** Ohio's Medicaid expansion covers a large portion of Warren's population, but enrollment gaps exist and re-enrollment after a lapse is a common problem. Understanding what Medicaid covers, how to use it effectively, and how to navigate the re-enrollment process when coverage lapses are practical healthcare skills.

**The Homestead Exemption.** Ohio reduces property taxes for homeowners 65 and over or permanently disabled. For an elderly homeowner on Social Security, this is worth several hundred dollars a year. Many eligible residents in Warren are not enrolled. Teaching this systematically — and helping members apply — is one of the higher-value single interventions the Society can deliver.

## Tenant Rights

A significant portion of Warren's members are renters in housing maintained poorly by landlords who know their tenants don't know what the law requires. The power imbalance is real and teachable.

The course covers: what Ohio landlord-tenant law requires of landlords in terms of habitability; what a renter's options are when a landlord fails to make legally required repairs; how to document conditions in writing; how withholding rent appropriately works under Ohio law; what constitutes illegal eviction; and when to contact Community Legal Aid in Youngstown, which provides free legal help for housing matters in Trumbull County.

This is not legal advice. It is legal literacy — knowing enough to understand your situation, document it correctly, and know when to call someone whose job is to help. Many members who have legal options don't pursue them because they don't know the options exist.

## Healthcare Navigation

How to use Medicaid effectively. What to bring to a doctor's appointment and how to describe symptoms clearly. What questions to ask and how to understand the answers. When an ER visit is warranted versus a nurse line call versus waiting for a scheduled appointment. How to understand a discharge summary. How to get a referral when your primary care provider isn't referring you.

This connects directly to the healthcare lane. The systems navigation curriculum and the healthcare lane's health education work overlap here — both teach members how to get more from the healthcare system that already exists. The member who teaches healthcare navigation in the education lane may be the same member who does it in the healthcare lane.

---

## The Credit Economy

The Society's own credit system has rules that members need to understand to participate in it fully. What demurrage is and how it works. What the balance ceiling means for how members hold credits. How capital calls work — what they are, what they fund, how members participate in them. How the allowance is calculated and when it's paid.

This is governance knowledge as much as financial knowledge. A member who understands the credit economy participates in the General Assembly differently when a capital call resolution comes to a vote. The numeracy curriculum (Phase 1.2) builds the mathematical foundation; the systems navigation course builds the institutional understanding.

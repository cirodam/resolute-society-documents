# Warren, Ohio — Community Care Landscape

## The Population

Warren's poverty rate and age profile produce a high concentration of residents who need ongoing practical assistance to manage daily life. The 40.3% Medicaid enrollment figure includes not just people who are poor but people who are poor and disabled — Medicaid is the primary payer for long-term care and disability services in Ohio. 18.5% of the population is over 65, a share that is growing as younger people leave. The opioid crisis has left households where the primary caregiver is a grandparent raising grandchildren, or where an adult child's addiction has created a functionally disabled household. Veterans with service-connected physical and mental health disabilities are dispersed across the city.

Ohio's disability rate runs significantly higher than the national average in counties with concentrated poverty and deindustrialization histories. Trumbull County is among those counties. The people with disabilities in Warren are not a marginal population. They are a substantial and concentrated part of the city's most vulnerable residents.

---

## How They're Currently Served

**Medicaid waiver programs.** Ohio's PASSPORT program provides Medicaid-funded home care services — personal care assistance, homemaker services, meal delivery — for elderly and disabled individuals who meet clinical and financial eligibility criteria. The program is real and helps real people. Its reach is limited by enrollment complexity, provider availability in Trumbull County, and the bureaucratic friction of a managed care system that many eligible residents don't know how to navigate.

**Community Action Agency.** Provides some home care and supportive services for low-income elderly and disabled residents in Trumbull County. Limited capacity.

**Family and informal networks.** The most common care arrangement for disabled residents in Warren is a family member — often a daughter, often doing it while also working and raising children — managing care informally, without compensation, without respite, and without any formal support structure. This arrangement is common, unstable, and invisible to any data system.

**Churches.** The Black church network in particular provides informal care through congregational relationships — checking on elderly members, providing transportation to appointments, organizing meals. This is real mutual aid that has operated for decades. It is not systematic and it cannot reach everyone.

## What Falls Through

The person who lives alone and has no family nearby. The person whose disability is invisible enough that neighbors don't notice until something goes wrong. The person who doesn't qualify for PASSPORT because their income is slightly too high or their functional limitations don't meet the clinical threshold, but who is nonetheless not managing independently. The person navigating addiction recovery who also has a physical disability and is falling through the cracks of every system simultaneously.

The common thread is isolation. Disabled residents in Warren who are isolated from family, from church community, from any consistent institutional presence are the people most likely to be in crisis before anyone knows to help. The healthcare lane identifies this as its foundational challenge. The community care lane is the specific institutional response to it.

---

## What This Means for the Society

A Society that does not systematically identify and support its members with disabilities is a Society that talks about care without practicing it. The community care lane is how the Society makes the covenant with its most vulnerable members concrete: you are known, your needs are understood, and this community will organize itself to meet them.

This is not a specialized service delivery program. It is the community doing what communities are supposed to do — knowing who is among them and what they need — organized through the ledger so that the labor of caring is compensated and the responsibility is distributed rather than falling on whoever happens to notice.

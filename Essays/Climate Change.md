# Climate Change

---

## Opening

- The Society's position on climate change is not complicated: it is happening, it is human-caused, and the consequences are serious. The evidence for this is not a matter of genuine scientific controversy. It is among the most thoroughly replicated findings in the history of modern science.
- But the essay does not stop there. The debate as it actually exists in public life is not primarily about the evidence — it is about institutional trust, economic interests, and the systematic manufacture of doubt. Those things are worth examining honestly.
- What this essay will do: state what the evidence shows, explain why a genuine-seeming debate exists despite settled science, engage honestly with the distinction between what is known and what is uncertain, and say what the Society's framework requires in response
- What the essay will not do: pretend the question is open when it is not, or pretend institutional and economic interests haven't shaped the public debate in specific and documentable ways

---

## I. What the Evidence Actually Shows

*To be developed*

- What is settled: the earth is warming, the warming is primarily caused by human greenhouse gas emissions, the consequences include sea level rise, increased extreme weather events, ecosystem disruption, and significant threat to human civilization as currently organized
- What has genuine uncertainty: precise timelines, regional effects, tipping point thresholds, feedback loop dynamics — the settled science does not claim precision on these and honest communication shouldn't either
- The distinction between settled science and uncertain science matters: overclaiming certainty on specific predictions creates vulnerabilities that manufactured doubt exploits
- The evidence base: not one institution, not one country, not one political tradition — converging evidence from thousands of independent researchers across decades

---

## II. Why the Debate Exists

*The manufactured doubt playbook*

- The fossil fuel industry has known about climate change since at least the 1970s — internal documents establish this
- The investment in manufactured doubt: funded think tanks, sponsored scientists willing to produce outlier findings, coordinated media strategies designed to create the appearance of scientific controversy where none existed
- This is the tobacco playbook applied to a larger problem — and it worked for decades
- The mechanism: you don't need to disprove the science. You only need to make people feel the question is unsettled. A population that believes scientists disagree will defer action indefinitely.
- What this means for the Scientific Studies essay: manufactured uncertainty is a real and documented phenomenon, and climate change is its most consequential application

---

## III. Institutional Trust — The Honest Complications

*To be developed*

- The institutional trust angle cuts in more than one direction
- Climate scientists and environmental institutions have sometimes overclaimed certainty on specific predictions — timelines that didn't hold, regional predictions that missed — which creates genuine vulnerabilities
- The honest communication standard the Society holds applies here: where genuine uncertainty exists, it should be named as such, even when doing so creates openings for manufactured doubt
- Economic interests have also shaped institutional positions from the other direction: the renewable energy industry, carbon credit markets, and certain research funding structures create incentives that can distort communication in the other direction
- Neither of these qualifications changes what the evidence shows about the fundamental question. They matter for how the Society communicates about it.

## IV. What the Society's Framework Requires

*To be developed*

- Axiom VI is direct: the living systems of the earth have integrity that exists independently of human valuation. The climate is not a resource to be managed. It is a system we depend on and are part of.
- Axiom V: no generation creates what it receives. The atmospheric stability that made human civilization possible was not built by anyone alive. We are burning it.
- Principle 27 (Ecological Stewardship): the default is non-interference. The burden of justification falls on those who would extract and disturb — not on those who would restrain. The burden has not been met.
- Principle 6 (Precaution): some decisions cannot be undone. The accumulation of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere is not reversible on human timescales. That asymmetry generates an asymmetric burden.
- The Society does not treat climate change as a political question. It treats it as an empirical reality with direct implications for its obligations under its own axioms.

## V. Honest Engagement with Genuine Uncertainty

*To be developed*

- The difference between "climate change is real" and specific predictions about when and where
- How to hold firm on the settled science while being honest about the uncertain edges
- Why epistemic honesty about uncertainty is not the same as creating false balance
- The gradient problem: the consequences of climate change exist on a gradient — some things are highly certain, others less so — and treating the gradient honestly is both intellectually required and practically important

## VI. What the Society's Response Looks Like

*To be developed*

- Not just acceptance of the evidence but active response: the ecological stewardship commitments in Principle 27, the holding-in-trust commitment in Principle 28
- The Society's land stewardship, food systems, and relationship to the living world as direct expressions of what the axioms require
- Honest about the limits of what one Society can do — and why that doesn't change the obligation
- The Society as a model: not fixing the climate, but demonstrating what a community that takes the evidence seriously actually looks like in its daily life

# What a Person Should Be Able to Do

These are the competencies a member of the Resolute Society is expected to develop and maintain as an individual — things a single person should be able to do with their body, their mind, and basic tools, without depending on a household or a division to do it for them.

The standard here is functional adult competence, not expertise. A person does not need to be a nurse to know how to stop bleeding. They do not need to be a chef to cook a meal from basic ingredients. They do not need to be a farmer to grow something. The expectation is that these capacities are held, practiced, and not lost.

The world this standard is designed for is one in which institutions are less reliable than they once were, supply chains break, and disruptions last longer than they used to. In that world, a person who has let these capacities atrophy becomes a burden to those around them faster than they otherwise would. A person who holds them remains capable and contributing regardless of what is working and what is not.

---

## Feed Themselves

- Cook a real meal from basic ingredients — not packaged food, not something that requires instructions on a box
- Know how to use dry staples: grains, legumes, dried foods
- Preserve food by at least one method: fermentation, drying, salting, or pickling
- Grow something — even a container or a small plot. The point is knowing how, not the scale

## Care for Themselves and Others

- Perform basic first aid: stop bleeding, treat a burn, recognize signs of a serious injury or medical emergency, know when something is beyond their ability to treat
- Know their own medical history, current medications, and any conditions that require management
- Know when to rest and how to recover — not push through everything as a point of pride
- Maintain basic physical fitness: walk significant distances, carry a load, do physical work without breaking down

## Maintain Their Belongings

- Mend clothing: basic hand sewing, darning worn fabric, replacing a button, fixing a seam
- Use basic hand tools: hammer, saw, screwdriver, knife, and know which task each is suited for
- Keep their tools in working order
- Know when something is fixable and when it is not

## Navigate and Situate Themselves

- Read a map and navigate without a phone
- Know their local environment: what the weather does, what the seasonal hazards are, what grows here, where water is
- Find and purify water by at least one method
- Build a fire safely and put it out completely

## Participate in Community Life

- Speak clearly in a group and make a case for what they think
- Listen to someone they disagree with and understand what that person actually believes before responding
- Engage in a conflict directly rather than avoiding it or routing it through others
- Participate in a democratic decision — including accepting outcomes they did not vote for
- Ask for help without shame

## Maintain Themselves Psychologically

- Sit with uncertainty without destabilizing
- Regulate themselves under stress well enough to remain useful to those around them
- Know their own emotional patterns well enough to recognize when they are being driven by them
- Maintain relationships that are honest, not just comfortable
